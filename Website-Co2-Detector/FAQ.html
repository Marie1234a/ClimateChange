Frequently Asked Questions

A company's CO₂ footprint measures the total greenhouse gas emissions directly and indirectly caused by the company's activities. It includes emissions from energy consumption, production, transportation, waste management, and other business processes. This metric allows the environmental impact of a company to be assessed and understood.

We determine emission data by analyzing publicly available information, corporate reports, life cycle assessments, and by collaborating with organizations specialized in environmental data. Additionally, we use recognized standards and methods for calculating emissions to ensure a reliable data foundation.

The emission data we provide is based on the most current information available. While we strive to calculate emissions as accurately as possible, some values may be considered estimates, especially when certain data is missing. We regularly update our information to enhance accuracy and reliability.

To reduce the CO₂ footprint, companies can take various actions, including the use of renewable energy, increasing energy efficiency, promoting sustainable transportation solutions, reducing waste, and encouraging the circular economy. Additionally, training employees to promote environmentally conscious work practices is crucial. For more information, feel free to subscribe to our newsletter.
